Affordable Housing on New York City-Owned Land: A look inside the city's recent housing RFPs

New York City's Department of Housing Preservation and Development (HPD) uses requests for proposals (RFPs) to allocate city-owned land to development teams to build affordable housing and community facilities across the boroughs. These projects often represent the most exciting and cutting-edge affordable housing developments in the city, as HPD's competitive selection process incentivizes developers to find creative ways to maximize affordability and the number of units, propose beautiful and contextual building design, advance sustainability and even regenerative building features, and propose unit types and amenities that will enhance the communities in which they are located. This map visualizes the RFPs that are open/under review as of May 2026, or designated by HPD since 2019. (Designated means HPD has selected a proposal). Each project will take several years, sometimes as many as 5-10, to complete, from time of designation. Designation itself can take 1-2 years. While these projects add critical units to the city's housing supply, the map also conveys how much farther the city has to go to meet its housing needs, with only 22 projects designated or under review since 2019.
