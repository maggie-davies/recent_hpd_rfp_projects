This project maps the New York City Department of Housing Preservation & Development (HPD)'s recent Requests for Proposals (RFP)s to dispose city-owned land to development teams to build affordable housing. The map draws from an author-created dataset of RFP projects taken from HPD's website and online sources, to map and explore projects that have been designated since 2019. Each of these projects went through a rigorous selection process, competing against development teams from around the world, in order to capture HPD designation. 

In addition to housing, some of the projects detail symbiosis with additional city goals like: senior housing development, development of housing on NYCHA (New York City Housing Authority) land, and creation of art and cultural facilities that often require separate fundraising processes to finance. Passive House design, regarded as one of the most stringent sustainable building design standards, is common, especially among the more recent of these projects.

The map is interactive. Hovering on each project marker pulls up a pop-up with an image and the name of the HPD RFP. Clicking unlocks further information, such as the name of the winning proposal, the development team and architect behind the project, more photos, and a link to the project website, as well as how many units are proposed, and highlights of the project's design and function.
